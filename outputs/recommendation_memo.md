# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

The company conducted an A/B experiment to evaluate a new onboarding and activation campaign. The treatment group demonstrated a statistically significant improvement in paid conversion rate compared with the control group. However, several guardrail metrics indicate potential risks that should be considered before a full rollout.

---

## North Star Metric

The selected North Star metric was Paid Conversion Rate, defined as the proportion of users who converted to a paid subscription.

---

## KPI Tree Summary

The North Star metric was supported by three primary drivers:

- User Acquisition Quality
- User Activation and Engagement
- Revenue Generation

Guardrail metrics included:

- Refund Rate
- Support Ticket Rate
- Average Days to Convert

---

## Experiment Result Summary

Compared with the control group, the treatment group achieved:

- Higher landing page visit rate
- Higher trial start rate
- Higher onboarding completion rate
- Higher paid conversion rate
- Higher engagement score
- Faster conversion

---

## Hypothesis Test Interpretation

A one-tailed two-proportion z-test was performed on the paid conversion rate.

- Z-statistic = 3.264
- P-value = 0.000549

Since the p-value was less than 0.05, the null hypothesis was rejected. The treatment group showed a statistically significant improvement in paid conversion rate.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

Positive observations:

- Engagement score increased.
- Users converted faster.
- Conversion improved across all regions.

Risks identified:

- Support ticket rate increased significantly.
- Refund rate increased slightly.
- Average revenue per converted user decreased substantially.

---

## Segment-Level Insight

All regions experienced higher conversion rates under the treatment group. No major regional decline was observed.

---

## Final Recommendation

### Continue Testing

Although the treatment significantly improved conversion, the increase in support tickets and reduction in revenue per converted user indicate possible quality issues.

A segment-specific rollout or additional experimentation is recommended before launching the treatment to all users.

---

## Risks and Limitations

- Revenue quality declined.
- Support burden increased.
- Results are based on a single experiment period.
- Long-term retention and customer lifetime value were not evaluated.

---

## Next Steps

1. Investigate reasons behind higher support ticket rates.
2. Analyze customer quality and retention.
3. Run additional experiments on selected user segments.
4. Monitor refund rates and revenue quality before a full rollout.
